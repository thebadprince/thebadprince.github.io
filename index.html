Hi, I'm Jomi Cubol. I'm a product designer based in New York. My mission is to help build things that have a meaningful impact on society, creativity, and culture.

About

I love software, the internet, design, and technology. Every day I marvel at how these layers allow us to be smarter, more creative, and more connected. They also allow us to be more aware, more expressive, and more ourselves. And I hope to add my contribution, to invent and help build new, insightful, beautiful, and lasting products that give more people more access, more opportunity, and more things that makes them feel good.

You can check out my daily blog, read my columns, browse my reading list, listen to my SoundCloud, follow me on Twitter and Medium, or scroll below for some of my most recent work.

Feel free to reach out at jomi@thebadprince.com!

Work

Apploi Mobile 2.0 — Mobile interface and user experience design for Apploi, a job ecosystem seeking to reinvent the application process. The goal was to make it consistent with the newly designed and repurposed web consumer application, as well as increase engagement, repeat usage, and focus on Search as the core action and granular filtering for enhanced personalization.
View project details.

Apploi Consumer Web / Project Churchill — Preliminary research, design sprints, system design, prototyping, and user testing to rethink Apploi's online job application process from the ground up. The objective was to make applying for a job as easy, effective, and enjoyable as possible to increase success rates and foster organic usage and growth. Most importantly, we wanted to radically improve the user's entire job searching and application experience.
View project details. | View finished product.
In collaboration with: C. Cassidy, G. Narich, T. Gebauer, M. Raton

Apploi Enterprise Dashboard / Project Truman — Information architecture and prototyping for Apploi's enterprise dashboard used by hiring managers and business owners. The goal was to improve the client side experience to make it easier to do individual actions (add jobs, see candidates, create templates) and team actions (permissions, administration, upgrades, etc.).
View project details. | View finished product.
In collaboration with: T. Gebauer, G. Narich, M. Raton, S. Hanson
