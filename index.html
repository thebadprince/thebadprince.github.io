Things I believe in

  • We are at the service of our users. A great product solves a real problem for real people in a very distinct way and helps them become tangibly empowered because of it.
  • Most of the things we'll make will suck. And that's okay. We need to churn out a lot of solutions in order to get to a great one. Having the persistence to get there and throwing out work is part of the game.
  • Feedback is oxygen. Until what we're trying to build touches real people, all we have are a bunch of untested assumptions. Learning is the goal, not the artifact in itself.
  • Culture is everything. A team's creative output and execution depends on the organizational infrastructure around it. We can always do our part through enthusiasm, respect, and encouragement.
  • Conviction is the secret weapon. In our quest to provide a unique and differentiated supply for a market demand, a unified belief in what we're building and why we're doing it is our competitive advantage.
